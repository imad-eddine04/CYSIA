Artificial intelligence (AI) is reshaping how people work, learn, and communicate. It appears in search engines,
recommendation systems, navigation apps, and automated customer support. Instead of being programmed with fixed
rules for every situation, many AI systems rely on machine learning, where algorithms learn patterns from data.
This approach can improve performance over time as more data becomes available.

Deep learning is a subfield of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers. These networks can
learn complex representations from raw inputs such as images, audio, and text. In healthcare, AI can assist doctors
by detecting patterns in medical images and supporting clinical decisions. In finance, it can help detect fraud,
estimate risk, and monitor unusual transactions. In industry, AI can optimise supply chains and predict equipment
failures before they happen.

However, AI also introduces challenges and risks. If training data is biased or incomplete, models may produce
unfair outcomes. Privacy is another major concern: collecting and storing large volumes of data can expose
sensitive personal information. Some AI systems are difficult to interpret, making it hard to understand why a
model produced a particular prediction. For safety-critical applications, transparency and careful validation are
essential.

Large language models can generate text, summarise documents, and answer questions. They can be useful for writing,
translation, tutoring, and coding assistance. Yet they may also produce hallucinations: confident statements that
are incorrect. They can reflect stereotypes found in training data, and they may be misused to generate misleading
content. For these reasons, evaluation, monitoring, and human oversight are important.

Responsible AI aims to maximise benefits while reducing harm. It includes fairness, accountability, transparency,
robustness, and security. Good practice involves testing models across different groups, documenting limitations,
and protecting user data. In the future, AI will likely become more integrated into daily life, so society needs
both technical solutions and clear policies. Collaboration between researchers, engineers, organisations, and 
citizens is key to building trustworthy AI.

Artificial intelligence is transforming the way people live, study, and interact with technology. It is used in search engines,
recommendation platforms, intelligent assistants, and automated support systems. Rather than relying only on fixed and predefined
rules, modern AI systems depend on machine learning algorithms that identify patterns from large datasets. As more data becomes 
available, these systems can continuously improve their performance and adapt to new situations.
